This is 'Puchero'

In any region of Argentina, there is a dish that cannot be missing, it is the delicious stew originating from the Spanish colonizers, with modifications that make it a typical Argentine dish. It is a delicious osso buco and vegetable soup, which you can serve all together or on the one hand the broth and on the other the meat and vegetables. The preparation is a bit laborious, but it is a simple recipe and be sure to prepare it especially for those winter days.

Here we go, we need only those ingredients:

  • 1 kilogramo de Osobuco
  • 3 Papas
  • 2 Batatas
  • 2 Zanahorias
  • 2 Dientes de ajo
  • 1 Rama de puerro
  • 1 Rama de apio
  • 1 Rama de perejil
  • 1 Trozo de calabaza
  • 2 Cebollas
  • Agua
  • 1 pizca de Sal

How to do it:

  1. Chop the osso buco meat and place it in a pot with water and salt. You take this to the fire and let it cook over high heat. You remove impurities from the meat that rises to the surface with a slotted spoon.
  2. Cut the vegetables into medium pieces, larger than small. You add the vegetables little by little in the pot, after the first boil you start with the celery, parsley, leek garlic, onions and carrots.
  3. Add more water if necessary and after a few minutes, add the pumpkin, potatoes and finally the sweet potatoes. You keep cooking by lowering the heat to medium and seasoning to your taste.
  4. The Argentine stew is ready when the meat and vegetables are tender. Serve the broth and meat separately with the vegetables and garnish with a little fresh parsley.

Hope you enjoy!
